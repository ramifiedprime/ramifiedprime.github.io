Introduction to Smith's Methods

    Peter Koymans will visit the University of Bristol from 13th-24th April 2026, during which time he will give a lecture series on the powerful methods first introduced by Alexander Smith to tackle the Cohen--Lenstra--Gerth conjecture on class groups of imaginary quadratic fields and Goldfeld's conjecture on quadratic twists of elliptic curves.

    Abstract

    In 2017,  Alex Smith introduced a powerful new method to obtain the distribution of class groups and Selmer groups in twist families. Since then, these methods have been used to resolve or make progress on several longstanding conjectures, such as the congruent number problem, Goldfeld’s conjecture (conditional on BSD), Gerth’s conjecture on class groups, Nagell’s conjecture on the negative Pell equation (later refined by Stevenhagen), and the problem of obtaining the best bounds for representing integers as sums of two rational cubes. In this lecture series, we will present in detail how to obtain the distribution of the 8-rank of the class group of imaginary quadratic fields. Time permitting, we will also sketch how to generalize this to all 2^k-ranks. Our treatment will be based on several recent ideas that not only significantly simplify the original method but also yield substantially stronger error terms.
